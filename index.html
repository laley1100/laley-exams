1) Therapeutic Communication (1–60)

1) Therapeutic communication is best defined as
A. Casual conversation between paramedic and patient
B. A planned, deliberate, professional act using communication techniques
C. Giving medical instructions only
D. Talking to calm the patient
ANSWER: B
2) Shared understanding of information helps to
A. Achieve desired patient-care goals
B. Reduce documentation
C. Save time only
D. Limit patient interaction
ANSWER: A
3) Using silence in communication allows the patient
A. Feel ignored
B. Think and gain insight
C. Lose interest
D. End the conversation
ANSWER: B
4) Exploring is best described as
A. Changing the subject
B. Delving further into a topic
C. Giving medical advice
D. Summarizing data
ANSWER: B
5) Clarification is used when the paramedic wants to
A. Check understanding
B. Challenge the patient
C. Give instructions
D. End communication
ANSWER: A
6) Example of clarification is
A. “Why did you do that?”
B. “Tell me everything again.”
C. “What do you mean by ‘sicker than usual’?”
D. “You are wrong.”
ANSWER: C
7) Reflecting focuses on
A. Only medical facts
B. Client’s feelings and content
C. Paramedic’s opinion
D. Documentation
ANSWER: B
8) Functional-professional touch is used primarily for
A. Clinical care
B. Emotional bonding
C. Social greetings
D. Intimacy
ANSWER: A
9) When explaining procedures, paramedics should consider
A. Only time constraints
B. Documentation only
C. Personal opinions
D. Age and language barriers
ANSWER: D
10) Therapeutic impasses are best described as
A. Successful interactions
B. Blocks in the paramedic-client relationship

D. Communication goals
ANSWER: B
11) Communication failures in clinical settings mainly lead to
A. Improved teamwork
B. Faster discharge
C. Adverse patient events
D. Reduced workload
ANSWER: C
12) Communication is best described as
A. One-way process
B. Two-way process
C. Written only

ANSWER: B
13) Factor negatively affecting handover
A. Noise and interruptions
B. Clear documentation
C. Structured tools
D. Team collaboration

A. Equipment transfer

D. Financial records

15) NOT a benefit of handover
A. Accurate transfer
B. Patient safety
C. Increased workload

16) Recommended pause in critical handover
A. 5–10 sec
B. 20–30 sec
C. 1–2 min

17) SBAR stands for
